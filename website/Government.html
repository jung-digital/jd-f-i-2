  • Intermodal Freight Technology Working Group (IFTWG) [Expand]

    The Intermodal Freight Technology Working Group (IFTWG) is a public-private partnership focused on the identification and evaluation of technology-based options for improving the efficiency, safety, and security of intermodal freight movement. Working from this common goal, the IFTWG engages in efforts to marry industry and government priorities in a way that leverages collective experience and shared investment.

    The IFTWG accomplishes this goal by:

    • Cooperatively analyzing the intermodal freight transportation system to identify physical and information exchange choke points
    • Actively engaging leaders of industry, government, academia, and consulting in the open exchange of ideas
    • Exploring the feasibility of creative solutions to high-priority challenges across all modes
    • Developing and executing pilot technology demonstrations through public-private partnerships
    • Sharing information, best practices, and lessons learned with the freight community at large

  • Cross-Town Improvement Project (C-TIP) [Expand]

    The Cross-Town Improvement Project (C-TIP) was first conceived in the fall of 2004 at an Intermodal Freight Technology Working Group (IFTWG) meeting in San Antonio, and has since grown in both size and stature. The first iteration of the project consisted only of a high-level concept that incorporated an 'intermodal move database' for coordinating cross-town traffic to reduce empty moves between terminals, and loosely defined ideas for tracking intermodal assets and distributing information to truckers wirelessly. Through the application of the IFTWG's project vetting and preliminary benefit cost assessment methodology, the C-TIP project grew to incorporate all of the components below:

    Intermodal Move Exchange (IMEX)
    Facilitate the exchange of load data and availability information between railroads, terminal operators and trucking companies. The primary function will be to allow collaboration on defining pickup and delivery schedules and locations that maximize the potential for linking moves, and eliminating bobtail and empty moves.

    Chassis Utilization Tracking (CUT)
    Provide a means for chassis owners and users to accurately account for asset use, which is crucial for the allotment of fees, and to maintain the proper balance of chassis to support cross-town and other container deliveries.

    Real-Time Traffic Monitoring (RTTM)
    Provide a means for up-to-the minute information regarding roadway conditions, travel speeds, and predicted travel times to be captured and passed along to the trucking community. Using a combination of traditional roadway sensors, traffic probes (i.e., vehicles that report their progress while traveling on the roadways), and third party providers, RTTM will provide the traffic information necessary for drivers and dispatchers to make informed decisions regarding routing and departure times.

    Dynamic Route Guidance (DRG)
    The dynamic route guidance (DRG) engine of RTTM will utilize inputs from RTTM and a Geographic Information System (GIS) source along with simulation tools, to act as an intelligence tool to provide real-time visual routing around congested areas.

    Wireless Drayage Updating (WDU)
    Provide a means to wirelessly and inexpensively exchange information with drivers regarding trip assignments, traffic congestion information, trip status, and location information through a truck-mounted driver interface device (T-MDID).

    • International Express Trucking (IXT) [Expand]

      --Case Study--

      International Express Trucking (IXT) is an intermodal trucking company located in Kansas City, MO. IXT takes pride in providing a quality, dependable service tailored to fit individual company needs.

      Currently, IXT uses printed paperwork that is passed on to the drivers to dispatch orders. This is either handed to the driver or faxed. During this case study the dispatched orders will be recieved through a phone application. The driver will use the phone to request or refresh the daily dispatch. The phone will send a message to the server containing the driver ID, password, carrier and location. The server will send back the daily dispatch. The phone will delete any previous daily dispatch and store the new one. The phone will store the daily dispatch and display it to the driver on request. Unless the driver requests a refresh the display will always show the most recent daily dispatch. The phone will display a summarized list of all stops to the driver. The driver may select one of the stops to view details for that stop. The driver will be able to re-order how shipments are displayed in the daily dispatch. This order will remain in place until the driver refreshes the daily dispatch or does another re-order. The driver will use the phone to send status updates as stops are completed. First, the driver will select the stop he is working on. Depending on the type of stop and the profile settings the phone will display a screen in which the driver can change a small number of values and the date/time. The phone will send a message to the server containing the stop ID and status information.

      --Assignment--

      • The contractor will develop wireless applications using the current Cross Town Improvement Project Intermodal Exchange (IMEX) module that will dynamically schedule pick-ups as new order requests are received.
      • This application will be used by drivers to exchange information between their phone and the motor carrier's work management system.


      --Mission--

      • Productivity advantages by enhancing existing dispatching methods by providing additional information necessary to dispatch orders.
      • Reduce bobtail which affects pollution, road congestion, road conditions, and cost of operation.
      • Sustained use of the project software after the pilot test concludes.

  • Electronic Freight Management (EFM) [Expand]

    The Electronic Freight Management (EFM) initiative is a USDOT-sponsored project that applies Web technologies that improve data and message transmissions between supply chain partners. It promotes and evaluates innovative e-business concepts, enabling process coordination and information sharing for supply chain freight partners through public-private collaboration.

    The goal is to provide a mechanism for sharing supply chain freight information that is simpler, cheaper, and more efficient to implement than traditional EDI, allows for all, but especially small to medium sized and less sophisticated, supply chain partners to access the information, and makes it easier to customize the flow of information between and among partners. Another goal of the initiative is to design a system to ensure that data is entered once but used many times, thereby eliminating data transcription errors. EFM aims to help companies replace paper trails with electronic information, freeing up human resources that were previously devoted to manual data entry.

    • Interdom LLC - Pride Logistics [Expand]

      --Case Study--

      Interdom LLC is a third party organization offering rail services to the international and domestic shipping communities. Initially founded to provide intermodal services to steamship lines with no intermodal department to utilize double-stack train services, Interdom LLC business has evolved to allow any small and medium size transportation entity to benefit from intermodal transportation. Their customer/partners include steamship lines, non-vessel operating common carriers (NVOCC), freight forwarders, customs brokers, and supply chain owners. Interdom LLC services are offered nationwide.

      The participants for this pilot are Interdom and Pride Logistics. To initiate a shipment, Interdom orders trucking services from Pride; Pride sends status information to Interdom. At the end of the shipment, Pride sends an invoice to Interdom. Today these processes take place via fax, Email and phone. During the EFM pilot they will be automated by exchanging information via web services.

      Interdom uses a system developed by Flatirons Two called LOTS. Pride uses a Microsoft Access Database system. The interface between the existing systems and the web services and clients will take place through database extracts in the form of text files. Some activity at one of the trading partners will trigger a process that will create a text files and then trigger the web service client. The client will use the text file to format the appropriate UBL xml message and invoke a web service at one of the trading partners. The web service will map the xml message to a text file and trigger a process that will update the database with the text file.

      --Assignment--

      • Interdom sends orders to Pride to move containers from origin to an intermodal rail terminal. This will be automated by using the EFM book consignment service.
      • Status, rail arrival, pre-note, accept/reject, delivery, and invoice communications will also be automated via EFM web services.


      --Mission--

      • Improved accuracy of data exchanges - including more complete information with respect to status, order, and invoices.
      • Improved timeliness of all data exchanges.
      • Reduced costs associated with conducting entirely manual exchanges.
      • Reduced costs associated with correcting incomplete or incorrect data.
      • Use of web services/automation of messaging reduces redundancy of effort.
      • Sustained use of the EFM package after the pilot test concludes.
